Bridging the Healthcare Gap in India

A project to improve healthcare access for underserved communities.

Problem Statement

India’s healthcare system faces a stark divide—while urban centers offer world-class medical facilities, millions of Indians in rural and economically weaker urban areas lack access to even basic healthcare services.

Key Data Points

  • 600 million Indians (45% of the population) have limited or no access to quality healthcare (NITI Aayog, 2023).
  • Doctor-to-patient ratio: India has 1 doctor per 1,511 people, below the WHO-recommended 1:1,000 ratio. In rural areas, the ratio is 1:10,000.
  • 55% of healthcare costs are paid out-of-pocket, pushing 63 million people into poverty annually (Public Health Foundation of India, 2023).
  • Health insurance coverage is low—only 37% of Indians have any form of insurance (IRDAI, 2023).
  • Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) cause 60% of deaths in India, many of which are preventable with early diagnosis and better access to treatment.

Our Solution

We invite participants to design an innovative, scalable, and financially sustainable business model that improves affordable and accessible healthcare for underserved communities in India.

Key Features of Our Solution:

  • Leverage technology – AI, telemedicine, fintech, blockchain, or other innovations to enhance efficiency and service delivery.
  • Ensure financial sustainability – Develop a revenue model that makes the solution self-sustaining while keeping services affordable.
  • Optimize healthcare access – Solve inefficiencies in diagnostics, treatment, supply chains, or financing mechanisms.
  • Address affordability and last-mile reach – Provide low-cost, high-quality healthcare in remote or low-income areas.
  • Have a clear scalability plan – Show how the idea can expand beyond a pilot phase through partnerships or innovative strategies.
